# A/B Testing Suggestions

## Test 1: Hero Headline — Journey vs Proof
**A:** "From first kick to first travel team. One academy. One family."
**B:** "33 families. 5.0 stars. Zero asked twice."
Why: Journey appeals to aspiration. Proof appeals to risk-aversion. Both are strong for Solution-Aware parents. Test which drives more scroll depth.
Metric: Scroll depth past hero, CTA clicks
Impact: +15-30% engagement difference
Priority: HIGH

## Test 2: Primary CTA — "Book a Free Trial" vs "Save My Spot"
**A:** "Book a Free Trial — No Payment Required"
**B:** "Save My Spot — Free Trial Class"
Why: "Book" is action-oriented (confident parents). "Save" implies scarcity (loss-averse parents). Both include friction reducers.
Metric: CTA click-through rate
Impact: +5-15% clicks
Priority: HIGH

## Test 3: Programs Section — Age-First vs Story-First
**A:** Program cards with age range as primary text (scannable)
**B:** Current copy with narrative descriptions (immersive)
Why: Mobile parents may scan by age first. Test whether they want efficiency or storytelling.
Metric: Program page click-through, time on section
Impact: +5-10% program page visits
Priority: MEDIUM

## Test 4: Social Proof Placement — Below Hero vs Integrated in Hero
**A:** Separate social proof bar below hero
**B:** Star rating and review count integrated into hero subheadline
Why: Integrated proof reinforces the hero message immediately. Separate bar creates its own visual beat. Test which drives more trust at first impression.
Metric: Bounce rate, scroll depth
Impact: +3-8% bounce rate reduction
Priority: MEDIUM

## Test 5: Exit-Intent — Email vs Phone Number
**A:** Ask for email
**B:** Ask for phone number
Why: Phone enables SMS follow-up (higher open rates). Email is lower friction. Test which captures more leads and which converts to trial bookings.
Metric: Form completion rate, trial booking rate
Impact: +10-20% form completions (email likely wins), but phone may convert higher downstream
Priority: LOW — test after hero and CTA are optimized
